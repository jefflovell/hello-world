Inspiration & purpose

Small rules, visible emergence.

Conway's Game of Life shows how complex behavior can evolve from humble beginnings. The game runs on just two rules: cells are born with three live neighbors among the eight surrounding cells, and living cells survive if they have two or three live neighbors. Each state update represents a new generation where those rules are re-applied, giving rise to wonderfully rich emergent behavior based on the starting pattern and count of living cells.

This version turns that system into a compact simulation console with classic specimens, adjustable grid density, speed control, and direct drawing on the field. Explore well known patterns, play around with random generations, or draw cells directly on screen at any time to change the course of evolution.

Why this stack

Lightweight, but still expressive.

Game of Life is mostly grid state and rule evaluation, so Canvas 2D gives the piece enough rendering control without the weight of a WebGL layer. The result stays small and direct.

The interface focuses on inspection: choose a known seed, adjust the grid, change speed, step through generations, or draw cells into the field by hand.

The live simulation and source code are available on GitHub.
